## Why this exists

Modern software has a habit.

It wants your files.  
It wants your attention.  
It wants to reorganize your life into *its* system.

Óculo exists because we think software can do less, and do it better.

Your documents already live somewhere.  
Your folders already mean something.  
Your system already has structure.

So instead of importing, syncing, capturing, indexing everything into yet another ecosystem…

**Óculo simply looks where you point it.**

Nothing more.

It doesn’t own your library.

It doesn’t try to become your second brain.

It doesn’t ask you to reorganize your life around an app.

It just gives you a calm, fast, beautiful way to navigate what is already yours.

---

## A different philosophy of software

A lot of apps today are built around capture.

Capture users.  
Capture attention.  
Capture data.  
Capture ecosystems.

We believe software should be infrastructure, not addiction.

That means:

* no accounts  
* no subscriptions  
* no telemetry  
* no cloud dependency  
* no notifications  
* no feeds  
* no dark patterns  
* no engagement mechanics pretending to help you  

Software should feel like a tool on your desk.

Not a casino.

---

## Why open source matters

We believe software freedom matters.

Not as a slogan.

As a practical necessity.

If software handles your documents, your knowledge, your work, your thinking…

you should be able to know what it does.

You should be able to inspect it.

Fork it.

Modify it.

Preserve it.

Build on top of it.

The future of personal computing cannot belong entirely to closed ecosystems designed around extraction.

FOSS is one of the few spaces where software can still exist primarily for the benefit of its users.

Óculo is part of that tradition.

---

## What Óculo is

Óculo is a lightweight document library for macOS.

It scans an existing folder structure and turns it into a navigable library.

That’s it.

Your folders become categories.

Your documents stay exactly where they are.

Nothing gets moved.

Nothing gets copied.

Nothing gets rewritten.

Delete Óculo tomorrow and your library remains exactly as it was.

As it should.

---

## What makes it different

* Fully local  
* Zero telemetry  
* Read only by design  
* Keyboard-first navigation  
* Extremely fast document discovery  
* Optional local LLM search through Ollama  
* Uses your existing filesystem structure  
* No proprietary cloud services  
* No hidden data collection  
* No account system  
* No attention engineering  

---

## Who this is for

Óculo is probably for you if:

* you keep large personal libraries of PDFs, EPUBs, books or research papers  
* you use carefully organized folders and hate apps that force imports  
* you care about privacy and local-first software  
* you prefer keyboard driven workflows  
* you self-host things when possible  
* you use tools like Obsidian, Zotero, Calibre, DEVONthink or plain folders, but want something lighter  
* you believe your computer should belong to you  

---

## AI, but on your terms

Óculo has optional semantic search.

But there is an important distinction.

The app does not ship AI.

It does not connect to external APIs.

It does not send data anywhere.

If you want enhanced search, you can connect your own local Ollama instance.

No cloud.

No vendor dependency.

No silent data extraction.

And if you don’t want AI at all…

Everything still works perfectly.

## Por qué existe

El software moderno tiene una costumbre extraña.

Quiere tus archivos.  
Quiere tu atención.

Tu ordenador ya sabe organizarse.

Así que en lugar de importar archivos, crear bibliotecas propietarias o obligarte a adaptarte a otra aplicación…

**Óculo simplemente mira donde tú le dices.**

Nada más.

No intenta convertirse en tu segundo cerebro.

No te obliga a reorganizar tu sistema.

No captura tu información.

Simplemente te deja navegar, encontrar y abrir lo que ya es tuyo.

De forma rápida, bonita y sin fricción.

## Qué es Óculo

Óculo es una biblioteca ligera de documentos para macOS.

Escanea cualquier carpeta existente y la convierte en una biblioteca navegable.

Eso es todo.

Tus carpetas se convierten en categorías.

Tus archivos permanecen exactamente donde estaban.

Nada se mueve.

Nada se copia.

Nada se reorganiza.

Nada se modifica.

Si mañana borras Óculo, tu sistema sigue exactamente igual.

Como debería ser.

---

## Qué lo hace diferente

* completamente local  
* privacidad total  
* diseño de solo lectura  
* navegación centrada en teclado

* no recopila absolutamente ningún dato

## IA, pero bajo tus reglas

Óculo incorpora búsqueda semántica opcional.

Pero con una diferencia importante.

La aplicación no integra modelos propios.

No usa APIs externas.

No envía tus documentos a ningún servidor.

Si quieres mejorar la búsqueda, puedes conectar tu propia instancia local de Ollama.

Tu máquina.

Tu modelo.

Tus reglas.

Y si no quieres usar IA en absoluto…

la aplicación sigue funcionando exactamente igual.

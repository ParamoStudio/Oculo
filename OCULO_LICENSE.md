---

## Why this license exists

Óculo exists because we believe personal software has taken a wrong turn.

Too many applications now attempt to own what they were only meant to serve.

Files imported into proprietary systems.

Mandatory cloud services.

Software built around attention capture.

Telemetry normalized by default.

Deliberate dependency on closed ecosystems.

We believe in something different.

Your documents belong to you.

Your filesystem structure belongs to you.

Your computer should remain a personal space, not an extension of another company’s infrastructure.

Óculo was built precisely as a response to that trend.

A quiet library.

No accounts.

No telemetry.

No capture.

No systems engineered to turn tools into addictive products.

---

## Why we chose copyleft

Óculo was built on decades of shared work from the free software community.

It would be fundamentally contradictory to benefit from that open knowledge while allowing someone else to privatize the result.

This is why we chose copyleft.

Deliberately.

Not by default.

Permissive licenses such as MIT, BSD or Apache allow companies to take open work, improve it privately, and build proprietary derivatives without returning anything to the community.

That model works for many projects.

It does not work for this one.

Anyone building upon Óculo must preserve the same freedoms that made its existence possible.

---

## Why AGPL specifically

AGPL was chosen because its copyleft extends beyond distributed software.

If someone modifies this project and runs it as a service or network-accessible product, they must also publish the source code for those modifications.

There are no silent SaaS forks.

No possibility of taking collective work, wrapping it behind a subscription, and giving nothing back.

Collective work should remain collective.

## What this license prevents

This license prevents:

The line we draw is not against commerce.

It is against enclosure.
